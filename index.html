About Me

My name is Catherine Joy Calupas and I am a 19-year-old Junior Game Developer based in Vienna, Austria. I graduated 2020 with excellence at the HTL Spengergasse (Technical College in Vienna) in Game Design.

The most exciting aspect of developing a game is testing it. Of course, you have to test every line of code itself but playing through the whole thing without game-breaking bugs always leaves me satisfied.

Comments

Step 1 - Concept and Design

Before creating assets and scripts you need to start with the concept of the game. What design goals are you trying to reach? Which features help you create the experience? We usually start with a narrative/story and create a mood board with fitting artworks, games and animations.

Step 2 - High Concept

After nailing down the rough style and direction of the game, the next step is to create a detailed game document: genre, target audience, features etc. It is important to go into detail when describing the art style and the mechanics. That way every team member knows what should be achieved and planning sprints and milestone will be easier.

Step 3 - Project Management

Good planning may take up a lot of time at first but will end up saving time and energy on the long run. We use milestones like Pre-Production, Core Mechanics, Feature Complete and Gold to label the development stages and set up the goals for each milestone. Every week/fortnight we have a sprint meeting in which we discuss future goals and current progress.

Step 4 - Software Architecture

In this step the different objects, their functionality and their relationship to each other will be mapped out. I use component diagrams to visualize the code structure, which helps me keep track of the code structure.

Step 5 - Coding and Testing

Each line of code must be tested before moving on to the next line. Non-stop coding may lead to a string of errors that could have been prevented by thorough testing. When discovering bugs, a detailed description of the process is essential in order to recreate it.

Extras - Audio & Art

It is a privilege to have artists and audio designers that are proficient with the engine and can replace the assets themselves. However, sometimes it is necessary to have the programmer at hand in order to drag the right items into the inspector – especially early on in development.

Step 6 - Presskit & PR

The next steps would be creating a presskit and joining different competitions, posting the game on social media and telling friends and family about your game. Please check it out on Itch.io, Instagram @wheres.eleanor and Twitter @wheres_eleanor!
